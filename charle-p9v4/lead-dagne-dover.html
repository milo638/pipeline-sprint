Dagne Dover

Premium bag brand juggling commute, baby, and travel personas without persona-aware PDPs or lifecycle.

Revenue est. $45M Shopify Plus Priority: High

Why This Matters Now

Dagne Dover is pouring paid traffic into summer baby + travel drops, yet every shopper lands on the same bloated commute PDP. The first screen is an autoplay video and copy for the wrong persona, so conversion dies before the hero CTA even appears.

Killer Insight

No persona-specific PDP variant has ever been tested—commute, baby, and travel proof all stack in one template, so they have zero data on which story actually converts.

What’s happening

  • PDP gallery + copy are identical for Work/Baby/Travel, so no persona sees the right proof or bundle suggestions above the fold.
  • Influencer landing pages rely on 10 MB autoplay hero videos that take 6–7 seconds on mobile before a CTA renders.
  • Important specs (laptop sleeve size, bottle pocket depth) are buried in accordions, forcing pinching/scrolling before checkout.

What to fix

  • Drop a segmented hero toggle (Commute / Baby / Travel) so copy, imagery, badges, and bundle CTA swap within the very first screen.
  • Swap the autoplay video for a compressed image stack, add a sticky CTA bar, and insert a two-column organizer spec grid directly under price.
  • Rebuild influencer landing pages as stacked modules (hero, proof, bundle, CTA) under 1 MB so mobile users can convert without scrolling past dead space.
